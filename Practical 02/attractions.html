Top Attractions

Shaniwar Wada

A symbol of Pune's rich Maratha heritage, Shaniwar Wada is a historic fortification that dates back to 1732. Once the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire, this majestic structure is renowned for its impressive architecture, sprawling gardens, and the legendary tales of valor and intrigue that surround it. A visit to Shaniwar Wada offers a fascinating glimpse into the grandeur of Pune's past.

Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple

A famous temple in Pune dedicated to Lord Ganesha, founded by Dagdusheth Halwai over a century ago. Known for its splendid idol adorned with gold and jewels, it attracts millions of devotees. The temple is a focal point during the vibrant Ganesh Festival. A must-visit for spiritual seekers and tourists alike.

Sinhagad Fort

Perched atop a hill, Sinhagad Fort offers panoramic views and a glimpse into Pune's rich history. Famous for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, it showcases the valor of Maratha warriors. The fort is a popular trekking destination, blending natural beauty with historical significance. Ideal for history buffs and nature enthusiasts alike.
